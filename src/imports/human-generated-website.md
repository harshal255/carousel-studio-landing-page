To make a website look purely human-generated and avoid the sterile, predictable look of AI, you must focus on intentional imperfection, unique branding, and authentic depth.Here are the key points to remember, organized by category:Copywriting and ContentEmbrace personal anecdotes: Share specific, real-world stories that AI cannot replicate.Vary sentence structures: Mix ultra-short sentences with long, complex thoughts naturally.Use local slang: Include region-specific idioms, subtle humor, or industry jargon.Avoid AI buzzwords: Eliminate terms like "delve," "testament," "revolutionize," or "in conclusion."Take a bold stance: Express strong, unique opinions instead of neutral, AI-style summaries.Design and VisualsAvoid generic stock art: Use real photos of your team, office, or physical products.Ditch AI-art styling: Do not use hyper-perfect, glossy, or surrealistic AI-generated images.Introduce micro-asymmetry: Break rigid grid layouts slightly to feel hand-crafted.Custom illustrations: Use hand-drawn elements, rough sketches, or unique brand doodles.Intentional typography: Pair fonts with distinct personalities instead of standard tech fonts.UX and FunctionalityHuman-driven microcopy: Write error messages and button text with warmth and humor.Visible author profiles: Include detailed bios, social links, and real headshots for writers.Dynamic community areas: Feature active comment sections, forums, or raw user reviews.Imperfect case studies: Show the messy middle of projects, including failures and lessons.Hand-signed elements: Use digital scans of actual signatures or handwritten notes.
