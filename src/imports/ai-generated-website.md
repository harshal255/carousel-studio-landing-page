Websites that look AI-generated are often referred to in the tech and design community as "AI slop" or generic template designs. While there isn't just one single website, millions of new Software-as-a-Service (SaaS), portfolio, and crypto startups look identical because they are generated using the exact same underlying LLM data and component libraries (like shadcn/ui or Tailwind).You can instantly spot an AI-generated website by looking for these specific design dead giveaways:🎨 The "AI Aesthetic" Color & TypographyPurple and Indigo Gradients: Almost every generic AI site relies on a dark charcoal background paired with glowing purple, neon blue, or pink gradient accents.The "Inter" Font Monoculture: AI code assistants default heavily to the Inter font family. If the entire site uses just one single font for headers, body, and buttons, it is likely AI-generated.Extreme Rounded Corners: Buttons and container cards usually feature highly exaggerated border-radii (very soft, pill-shaped, or heavily curved edges).📐 Layout & Structure RepetitionThe Standard Bento Grid: Content is almost always arranged in a modular "Bento Box" style grid layout.Predictable Landing Page Formula: The page strictly follows a sequential script: a Hero section with text on the left and a floating dashboard graphic on the right, followed by a row of moving company logos, three pricing cards, and a standard FAQ accordion.Overused Animations: Heavy reliance on floating 3D elements, smooth background blurs (glassmorphism), and text fading in as you scroll.🖼️ Fake Assets & ContentGeneric Stock Imagery & Icons: AI builders frequently insert the same repetitive abstract textures, glowing globes, or unrelated tech illustrations.Bland, Over-Optimized Copy: The headings often sound like an AI trying too hard to sound corporate (e.g., "Revolutionize your workflow with next-generation AI-powered insights"), lacking any real brand voice or personality
